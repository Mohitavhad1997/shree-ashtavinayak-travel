Our story

Started with one route, one van, one promise

In 2011 we ran a single Ashtavinayak departure with fourteen people in a hired van. The promise then is the same one we keep now: show up on time, tell the truth about the route, and answer the phone.

How we work

Planning that starts with the calendar, not the brochure

We don't sell fixed packages first and fit you into them. Every enquiry starts with your dates, your group's pace and your budget — the itinerary is drafted around that, not the other way round.

Our coordinators have each run the routes they plan, on foot, by bus and by hired car — so when we say a temple queue is short before 7am, or a hill road is better avoided after dark, it's from having been there.

Our values

What doesn't change, even as routes do

Say the true thing

If a fort is closed for renovation or a road is under repair, you hear it from us before you book — not after.

Respect the pace

Older travellers, families with children and solo trekkers move differently. We plan for the group in front of us.

Stay reachable

A working phone number, answered by someone who knows your itinerary — for the length of the trip.
